# README and Credits

## What this is

**Ethical Travel: 30 Destinations Worth the Journey** is a Notion-ready notebook by **Gyro Governance**, an AI Safety Lab. It is the travel-facing companion to our framework, *Collective Superintelligence* and *The Human Mark*, distilled into thirty real-world places where you can apply it.

Each destination page includes:

- An **isometric adventure map illustration** with five anchor spots.
- **Practical essentials** drawn from Wikivoyage (the active community successor to Wikitravel).
- An **Ethical Compass** mapped to one of our four domains: Economy, Employment, Education, Ecology.
- A **copy-ready AI prompt** to paste into your own assistant.

## What’s in this Notebook

All thirty destinations ship with adventure-map artwork. Ten are full hand-painted photorealistic isometric maps; the remaining twenty are illustrated SVG-derived map cards in the same brand palette (color-coded by domain). The artwork prompts file lets you upgrade those twenty later without touching any Markdown.

## How to import into Notion

1. In Notion, create a new page (or open the page you want to import into).
2. Click **Settings & members → Settings → Import**, or use the **`...` menu → Import** on the page.
3. Choose **Markdown & CSV**.
4. Drag the **entire unzipped folder** into the import dialog. Notion will create the index page plus one sub-page per destination.
5. Re-link the images if Notion did not auto-attach them: each destination page references `../images/<slug>.png`. If the images do not appear, drag them into each page manually, or upload the `/images` folder to a Notion file-hosting page and update links.
6. Optional but recommended: turn the index page’s destination table into a **Notion database** (gallery view, with the map image as the cover) to make the notebook browsable and beautiful for buyers.

## How to sell this

- **Duplicatable template:** Once imported, share the top-level Notion page with **‘Allow duplicate as template’** enabled. Buyers click ‘Duplicate’ to copy the whole notebook into their own workspace.
