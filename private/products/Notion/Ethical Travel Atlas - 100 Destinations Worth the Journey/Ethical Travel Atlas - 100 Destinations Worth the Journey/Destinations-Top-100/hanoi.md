# Hanoi, Vietnam

Country: Vietnam
Region: Asia

![Hanoi, Vietnam](../Destinations-Top-100-assets/hanoi.jpg)

Hanoi (*Hà Nội*) is Vietnam's capital, a thousand-year-old city on the Red River where Confucian, French colonial, and Communist-era layers stack visibly on top of each other. A city of around 8.5 million, lake-centred, motorbike-busy, and the gateway to Ha Long Bay and northern Vietnam.

---

## 🧭 Step 1: Choices

### ✨ Why Visit

Hanoi is more atmospheric than Ho Chi Minh City and more politically and culturally weighted. The Old Quarter's 36 trade streets still mostly trade what their names say (silk, silver, tin). Hoan Kiem Lake is a working public space where Hanoians do tai chi at dawn and walk families at dusk. The Temple of Literature is Vietnam's first university, founded in 1070.

The city is also the political capital of contemporary Vietnam, and its museums (the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, the History Museum, the Vietnam Women's Museum, the Hoa Lo "Hanoi Hilton" prison) tell the country's twentieth century with directness rare in the region.

You come for the Old Quarter, the food (one of the world's great cuisines, more clearly than its southern sibling), the coffee, and as a base for Ha Long Bay, Ninh Binh, and Sapa.

### 🌍 Ethical Compass

- **💰 Economy.** Eat at street stalls and small *pho*, *bun cha*, and *bun rieu* shops in the Old Quarter and Hai Ba Trung district. Buy from small workshop owners in the 36 streets, not the tourist-front shops near St Joseph's Cathedral.
- **👥 Employment.** Tipping is not customary but increasingly appreciated; small change at restaurants and a small note for guides. Use Grab or Be (ride-hail) rather than informal *xe om* (motorbike taxis) unless you know the routine.
- **📚 Education.** Read at least one Vietnamese voice on the American War (what Vietnamese call the conflict the West calls the Vietnam War); Bao Ninh's *The Sorrow of War* is the literary classic. The Vietnam Women's Museum is essential.
- **🌱 Ecology.** Hanoi air pollution can be serious; check AQI daily and consider an N95 mask. Refill water from sealed sources; avoid tap. For Ha Long Bay, choose smaller licensed operators with current environmental certification.

## 🎒 Step 2: Preparation

### 🔍 Governance Management

- Most travellers need an **e-visa** through the official Vietnamese Immigration portal; verify your nationality's current status.
- **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum** has specific opening days (closed for two months annually for body maintenance) and a strict dress code; verify on the official portal.
- **Hoa Lo Prison Museum, Temple of Literature, and major museums** sell tickets at the gate; verify hours on official portals.
- For **Ha Long Bay** or **Lan Ha Bay cruises**, choose operators with current environmental and safety certification. The bay has had safety incidents historically; verify operator standards.
- For **train tickets** to Sapa or further south, book on the official Vietnam Railways portal or through a trusted agent.

### 📡 Information Curation

- **VnExpress International** and **Tuoi Tre News** (Vietnamese English-language outlets) for current events.
- The **Vietnam National Administration of Tourism** for official portals.
- A Vietnamese author: Bao Ninh's *The Sorrow of War*; Nguyen Phan Que Mai's *The Mountains Sing*; Nam Le's *The Boat*.
- A locally led food walking tour in the Old Quarter (Hanoi Street Food Tour, Hanoi Cooking Centre).
- **Wikivoyage Hanoi** for orientation.

### 🎯 Inference Interaction

- **You decide on the Mausoleum.** The dress code is strict (no shorts, no exposed shoulders, no cameras, no talking); the queue is long. Worth it for the experience of how Ho Chi Minh is presented today; not for the body itself.
- **You decide on Ha Long vs Lan Ha.** Ha Long Bay is the famous one and the most crowded; Lan Ha Bay (south, accessed from Cat Ba Island) is quieter, similarly beautiful, less industrial.
- **You decide on the Old Quarter scooter ride.** Crossing streets is the rite of passage; walk steadily and predictably, do not stop suddenly. A "walking tour" with a guide who shows you the technique is genuinely useful.
- **You decide on the egg coffee.** Hanoi's *cà phê trứng* is real, invented at Café Giảng. Try it where it was invented.
- **You decide on a guided cooking class.** Vietnamese cuisine deserves a half-day; the Hanoi Cooking Centre and similar offer market-and-meal experiences.

### 🔄 Intelligence Cooperation

Hanoi weather is four-season; northern Vietnam has real winter (December to February, cold and damp) and a summer monsoon (June to August). Major holidays (Tet Lunar New Year, Reunification Day, National Day) close many businesses for days.

Bring a soft plan. If air quality is red, indoor museums and cooking classes work. If a typhoon disrupts Ha Long Bay, the city itself has enough to fill several extra days. If Tet closes much of the Old Quarter, the parks and lake walks remain alive.

### 📍 Top 5 Anchor Spots

1. **Old Quarter and Hoan Kiem Lake.** Walk the 36 streets in the morning; tai chi at the lake at dawn; weekend pedestrian-only nights are excellent.
2. **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum complex + Temple of Literature.** A morning of political history followed by an afternoon of Vietnamese Confucian heritage.
3. **Vietnam Women's Museum and Hoa Lo Prison.** Two of the most thoughtful museums in Hanoi.
4. **Ha Long Bay or Lan Ha Bay overnight cruise.** Two-day-one-night minimum to actually experience it; three-day-two-night is better.
5. **Street food tour and a cooking class.** Bun cha at a market stall, pho at a corner counter, banh mi mid-morning, ca phe trung at Café Giảng.

### 🧰 Practical Essentials

- **Recommended Length.** Three to four days for Hanoi. Add two to three for Ha Long or Lan Ha; two to three more for Ninh Binh or Sapa.
- **Transport.** Walk the Old Quarter (carefully). **Grab** or **Be** for ride-hail (car or motorbike). The Hanoi Metro (Line 2A and Line 3) is new and limited but expanding. Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) is 30 to 50 minutes from the centre by taxi.
- **Daily Cost (per person).**
  - **Budget:** roughly VND 500,000 to 1,000,000 (about USD 20 to 40). Old Quarter hostel, street food, walking and Grab, two ticketed sites, one day-trip.
  - **Mid-range:** roughly VND 1,500,000 to 3,500,000 (about USD 60 to 140). Three-star hotel, mixed dining, all major sites, a Ha Long cruise (mid-tier), a cooking class.
  - **Higher-comfort:** roughly VND 5,000,000 and up. Sofitel Metropole or Capella Hanoi, fine dining at Apron Up or La Verticale, private guides, a higher-end Ha Long cruise (Heritage Cruises, Stellar of the Seas).
- **Booking Notes.**
  - **e-Visa:** apply on the official Vietnam Immigration portal.
  - **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum:** strict dress code, no cameras, closed Mondays and Fridays, closed for body maintenance (verify dates).
  - **Tet Lunar New Year (late January or early February)** sees many businesses closed for a week; verify your dates.
  - **Ha Long Bay safety:** verify operator's recent inspections; smaller licensed boats are often better than the largest.
  - **Air quality** can be severe in dry-season months (October to March).
