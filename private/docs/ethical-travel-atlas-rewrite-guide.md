# Ethical Travel Atlas Rewrite Guide

For: Gyro Governance Ethical Travel
Purpose: Transform the current placeholder Atlas into a destination-specific, AI-assisted ethical travel planning system aligned with the site's philosophy.

## Overview

The current Atlas structure is good but the content is mostly placeholder text.

The goal is not to create another travel guide.

The goal is to create an AI-Empowered Ethical Travel Atlas that teaches travelers:

1. How to choose what matters.
2. How to prepare thoughtfully.
3. How to use AI responsibly.
4. How to verify with real people.

The Atlas should embody Collective Superintelligence:

- AI assists research.
- Humans make decisions.
- Local people remain valuable sources of knowledge.
- Travelers retain responsibility.

## Core Structure

Every destination must follow this exact structure:

```text
Step 1: Choices

Step 2: Preparation

Step 3: Delivery
```

These steps appear in every destination. Do not rename them.

## Step 1: Choices

Replace the current generic "Why It Is Mainstream" section.

### Why Visit

- Write 2 to 4 short paragraphs.
- Keep it destination-specific, concrete, and memorable.
- Explain why someone should care.
- Explain what makes the place distinctive.

Do not use generic text like "A major gateway destination with iconic sights."

### Ethical Compass

Every destination must contain all four domains with these exact labels:

- `Economy`
- `Employment`
- `Education`
- `Ecology`

Each bullet must be destination-specific, practical, and actionable.

## Step 2: Preparation

Use all four Gyroscope Protocol work categories by name only:

- Governance Management
- Information Curation
- Inference Interaction
- Intelligence Cooperation

These are Gyroscope Protocol categories (GGG Employment domain). Do not append THM suffixes (Traceability, Variety, Accountability, Integrity). Do not use shortened labels (for example Traceability, Variety, Accountability, or Adaptation).

Do not mention The Human Mark in destination pages.

Do not use symbols in operation names.

### Governance Management

- 3 to 5 destination-specific bullets.
- Focus on assumptions that should be checked.

### Information Curation

- 3 to 5 destination-specific bullets.
- Include multiple source types and perspectives.

### Inference Interaction

- 3 to 5 destination-specific bullets.
- Clearly identify decisions the traveler owns.

### Intelligence Cooperation

- 1 to 3 short paragraphs.
- Emphasize trusting the traveler's judgment and local advice over algorithmic defaults when conditions change. The plan supports the traveler's judgment; it does not replace it.
- Do not shorten this operation to "Adaptation" or any label other than Intelligence Cooperation.

### Top 5 Anchor Spots

Replace placeholders completely.

Every destination needs five real locations:

```text

2. Place Name: short explanation.
3. Place Name: short explanation.
4. Place Name: short explanation.
5. Place Name: short explanation.
```

### Practical Essentials

Replace placeholders completely. Must include:

- Recommended Length
- Transport
- Budget (budget, mid-range, higher-comfort)
- Booking Notes (reservations, permits, seasonal limits, timed entry where relevant)

## Step 3: Delivery

Keep this section name consistent:

```text
Step 3: Delivery
```

### AI Prompt Rules

- Every prompt must begin with `Please`.
- Every prompt must use:
  - Step 1: Choices
  - Step 2: Preparation
  - Step 3: Delivery
- Step 2 must explicitly include:
  - Governance Management
  - Information Curation
  - Inference Interaction
  - Intelligence Cooperation
- Every prompt must end with verification guidance that asks to verify with official sources and local people.

## Writing Style Rules

- Tone: warm, practical, respectful, encouraging.
- Avoid guilt-driven writing and lectures.
- Use short sentences, concrete examples, direct recommendations.
- Avoid academic language and abstract theory.
- Do not use em dash or en dash characters.
- Prefer "to" in ranges and spell percentages in words in body text.

## Quality Check Before Completion

Each destination must pass all checks:

- Uses the 3-step structure.
- Contains all 4 Ethical Compass domains.
- Contains all 4 Gyroscope operations.
- Contains 5 real anchor spots.
- Contains real practical essentials.
- Prompt starts with "Please".
- Prompt includes all 4 operations.
- Prompt includes verification reminder.
- No placeholder text remains.
- No em dash or en dash characters.
- Destination-specific throughout.

## Ultimate Goal

A traveler should be able to open any destination page and immediately understand:

1. Why the destination matters.
2. What ethical considerations exist there.
3. How to prepare thoughtfully.
4. How to use AI effectively.
5. How to verify information with real people.

If content could be copied onto another destination without changes, it is too generic and must be rewritten.
