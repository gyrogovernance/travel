## 🧠 SCALING RULES (For All 100 Destinations)

### 1. Non-City Adaptation

Roughly ten of the 100 destinations are natural areas, not cities: Great Barrier Reef, Serengeti National Park, Maasai Mara, Iguazu Falls, Patagonia, Machu Picchu, Kruger National Park, Banff, Victoria Falls, and a few others. They have no neighbourhoods, no metros, no historic old towns. Adapt the template:

- Rename `Transport` to `Getting There and Around`. Cover flights to gateway cities or towns, shuttle services, park entrance roads, boat or ferry access, and park-internal transport (game drives, shuttles, hiking).
- In Anchor Spots and bullet lists, replace `neighbourhoods` with `zones`, `regions`, `gates`, `trailheads`, or `sectors` as appropriate.
- Adjust **Recommended Length** to reality. The Serengeti is not three days. Patagonia is seven to fourteen. Machu Picchu is one day at the site but three to five in the wider region. Banff is three to seven. Great Barrier Reef is two to five depending on the gateway. Iguazu is two days (one on each side of the border).
- Practical Essentials should mention park entrance fees, permits (Inca Trail, Torres del Paine W trek, gorilla permits), seasonal closures, and conservation rules in place of city rentals and metro fares.
- Ecological pressure is the dominant theme for these destinations. Lead the Ethical Compass with Ecology if it fits.

### 2. No Hallucinated Prices, Laws, or Regulations

When an AI is uncertain about a price, a law, or a regulation, it will often invent one that sounds plausible. This kills trust. Hard guardrails:

- **Never state a specific ticket price** (for example, "entry costs 12 euros") unless the price is universally stable and widely documented. Default phrasing: "check current pricing on the official portal".
- **Never state a specific law, fine amount, quota, or restriction** unless it is well documented and recent. Default phrasing: "verify current rules on [official body] before booking".
- **Never invent a local regulation** to fill a bullet. If there is no genuine ethical concern in that category for that destination, write the bullet about a different real concern instead.
- **Year references are forbidden in body copy.** The Atlas is meant to last. Use "currently", "recent years", "in peak season", "during high summer", instead of "since 2025" or "as of 2026". The only acceptable place to write a year is inside the AI prompt where the reader fills in their travel month.
- When in doubt, point the reader to the named official source (ministry, park authority, tourism board) rather than asserting a fact.

### 3. Prompt Variable Brackets Stay Obvious

The brackets in the AI prompt are the seams the reader fills in. Keep them visually loud and consistent:

- Always uppercase inside the brackets: `[NUMBER]`, `[MONTH]`, `[WHO]`, `[INTERESTS]`, `[AMOUNT]`, `[DESTINATION]`.
- Square brackets only. No curly braces, no angle brackets, no italics, no inline code.
- When a bracket needs an example, put it inside the brackets after a comma: `[INTERESTS, e.g. classical history, food, neighbourhood walking]`.
- Never leave a bracket empty or with placeholder lorem text.

### 4. Hard Style Rules (Repeat at Top of Every AI Instruction)

> **HARD RULE: Do not use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) anywhere in the text.** Use commas, periods, or colons instead. Use "to" for ranges. Spell out "percent". Use "and" instead of "&". Always hyphenate "AI-Empowered".

AI assistants love to sneak em dashes back in, especially in longer outputs. Re-paste this line every single time.

### 5. Standard Footer (Identical on Every File)

Every destination file ends with the same two-line block, no variations:

```
---

Part of **Gyro Governance Ethical Travel: AI-Empowered Guides for Human Adventures**.

Explore more destinations, ethical domains, and AI prompts at [travel.gyrogovernance.com](https://travel.gyrogovernance.com/).
```

This turns the Atlas into a funnel whether it is shared free, gated behind an email, or sold as a Notion template.
